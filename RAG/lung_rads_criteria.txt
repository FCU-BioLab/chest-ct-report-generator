Lung-RADS Classification System (version 1.1)

Category 0 (incomplete):
- Prior CT studies were performed but are not available.
- Lungs incompletely imaged.
- Findings suggest inflammation or infection.

Category 1 (negative, <1% chance of malignancy):
- No lung nodules.
- Lung nodule(s) with specific findings favoring benign nodule(s), such as:
  - Complete calcification
  - Central calcification
  - Popcorn calcification
  - Calcification in concentric rings
  - Fat-containing nodules

Category 2 (benign appearance or behavior, <1% chance of malignancy):
- Juxtapleural nodule <10mm mean diameter at baseline OR new and smooth, solid, oval, lentiform, or triangular.
- Solid nodule(s):
  - <6 mm at baseline
  - New nodule <4 mm
- Part-solid nodule <6 mm total mean diameter at baseline.
- Ground-glass nodule(s):
  - <30 mm
  - ≥30 mm and unchanged or slowly growing
- Subsegmental airway nodule at baseline or stable.
- Category 3 nodules that are stable or decreased at 6 months.
- Category 4B lesion that has a benign diagnosis on work up.

Category 3 (probably benign, 1–2% chance of malignancy):
- Solid nodule(s):
  - Between 6 and 8 mm at baseline
  - New nodule between 4 mm and 6 mm
- Subsolid nodule(s):
  - ≥6 mm total diameter with solid component <6 mm
  - New <6 mm total diameter
- Ground-glass nodule(s):
  - ≥30 mm on baseline CT or new
- Atypical thick-walled lung cyst with enlarging cystic component (mean diameter)
- Category 4A lesion, stable or decreased in size at 3-month follow-up (excluding airway nodules)

Category 4A (suspicious, 5–15% chance of malignancy):
- Solid nodule(s):
  - ≥8 mm to <15 mm at baseline
  - Growing nodule(s) <8 mm
  - New nodule 6 mm to <8 mm
- Part-solid nodule(s):
  - ≥6 mm total diameter with solid component ≥6 mm to <8 mm
  - New or growing <4 mm solid component
- Segmental or more proximal airway nodule at baseline
- Thick-walled cyst OR multilocular cyst at baseline OR thin-/thick-walled cyst that becomes multilocular

Category 4B (very suspicious, >15% chance of malignancy):
- Stable or growing airway nodule, segmental or more proximal
- Solid nodule(s):
  - ≥15 mm at baseline
  - New or growing, and ≥8 mm
- Part-solid nodule(s):
  - Solid component ≥8 mm
  - New or growing ≥4 mm solid component
- Atypical pulmonary cyst:
  - Thick-walled cyst with increasing wall thickness/nodularity OR growing multilocular cyst
  - Multilocular cyst with increased loculation or new/increased opacity (nodular, ground glass, or consolidation)
- Solid or part-solid nodule growing slowly over multiple screening exams

Category 4X (very suspicious, >15% chance of malignancy):
- Category 3 or 4 nodules with additional features or imaging findings that increase the suspicion of malignancy, including:
  - Spiculation
  - Ground-glass nodule(s) that double in size in 1 year
  - Enlarged regional lymph nodes
- For new large nodules on annual repeat screening CT, a 1-month LDCT may be recommended to address potentially infectious or inflammatory conditions.

Modified categories:
- [X]S (e.g., "3S") if there is a clinically significant or potentially significant non-lung cancer finding.
- (Removed) [X]C (e.g., "3C") for patients with prior diagnosis of lung cancer.

Changes in 2022:
- Up to 9% of lung cancers may present as atypical pulmonary cysts.
  - Classify as category 3, 4A, or 4B depending on the most concerning feature (wall thickening, nodularity, multilocularity, opacity, or interval growth).
- Juxtapleural replaces perifissural as descriptor for typical intraparenchymal lymph nodes.
- Endobronchial nodules are now called "airway nodules", concerning if in segmental/proximal location or showing growth.
- Nodule growth is defined by mean diameter change at 12-month interval.
- Ground-glass nodules that grow ≤1.5mm over 12 months are category 2; higher growth or new solid component triggers upgrade.
- Follow-up timing is based on finding severity.
- Potentially benign infectious/inflammatory findings:
  - May be assigned to Category 0 (e.g., >6 new nodules, rapid 8mm nodule, consolidation).
  - Reassess at 1–3 months and reclassify based on most suspicious feature.
  - Tree-in-bud nodules <3cm or new GGN <3cm may remain based on size criteria.
  - Multiple segmental abnormalities without proximal lesion may be Category 4.

Recommended follow-up:
- Category 0: Comparison with prior studies before assignment
- Category 1: Annual LDCT
- Category 2: Annual LDCT
- Category 3: 6-month LDCT
- Category 4A: 3-month LDCT, consider PET-CT if solid component ≥8 mm
- Category 4B / 4X: Chest CT ± contrast, PET-CT and/or biopsy depending on risk

Practical Notes:
- Measure nodules in lung window
- Report mean diameter to 1 decimal point (long + short axis ÷ 2)
- Growth = increase in mean diameter ≥1.5 mm
- Assign Lung-RADS category based on most suspicious nodule
- Perifissural nodules <10 mm with smooth oval/triangular shape = category 2
- Category X is written as “Lung-RADS category X”
